02 / 07 — THE PROBLEM

The Problem

A silent, physical attack on a digital system

As QR payments scale, so does a new form of fraud: QR Code Overlay Attacks. Criminals exploit the physical nature of static QR displays by printing their own codes, pasting them over legitimate business codes, and redirecting payments into their own accounts. No hacking. No software breach. Just timing and access.

The core vulnerability In fast-paced environments — cafés, takeaways, retail counters — staff rely on trust and speed. Customers confirm payment, staff move on, and operations continue. Until reconciliation. By the time discrepancies are noticed, transactions are gone, customers are unreachable, and losses are absorbed by the business.

The Insight

The weakest point isn't digital — it's physical

Payment platforms are secure. Encryption is strong. Systems are reliable. But the last inch — the physical QR display — is completely exposed. A printed square on a counter becomes easy to copy, easy to replace, and easy to exploit. The system is digitally secure, but physically vulnerable.

The Solution

Anti-QR Fraud Display Technology — securing payments at the point of interaction

We designed a system that doesn't rely on software updates or user behaviour. Instead, it eliminates fraud at the physical layer. Five core innovations work together to make overlay attacks visually obvious and practically impossible.

Core innovation: non-standard geometry Traditional QR displays are flat and predictable. Ours are intentionally uneven and asymmetrical. This breaks the flat surface criminals depend on, makes overlay attempts visibly misaligned, and forces tampering to look "off" instantly.
Brand + shape lock integration Each unit is custom-integrated with business identity. Non-rectangular QR display zones with embedded branding around the code mean that to successfully fake it, a criminal must replicate branding, match complex shapes, and align perfectly on an uneven surface. The effort outweighs the reward.
