04 / 10 — PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

Dev Process

Industry-standard new product development — from insight to market

LINKPOD™ was developed using a structured Stage-Gate NPD (New Product Development) process — a proven industry framework that ensures quality, feasibility, and market alignment at every phase before committing to the next stage of investment.

01
Idea Generation & Opportunity Screening Problem identification through user observation: MiFi routers lack physical integration with smartphones. Opportunity assessed against market gap, technical feasibility, and commercial viability using a weighted scoring matrix (technical fit 40%, market attractiveness 35%, strategic alignment 25%).
02
Concept Development & Testing Three concepts developed: (A) adhesive pouch, (B) magnetic snap system, (C) rubber corner-grip system. Concept tests conducted with 40 target users across remote worker, student, and traveller segments. Magnetic and grip concepts advanced based on preference scores and usability ratings ≥7/10.
03
Business & Technical Feasibility Analysis Material selection — PU leather (premium feel, durability, weatherproof) vs matte rubber (tactile grip, everyday resilience). BOM costing, margin analysis, MOQ break-even modelling, and manufacturing pathway assessment: manual cast for ≤50 units, factory tooling for 500+ units.
04
Prototype Development (Iterative) Three prototype rounds (P1: foam mock, P2: 3D-printed ABS with PU wrap, P3: cast silicone with final material). Key iterations: magnet strength calibration (N45 neodymium, 1.2 kg pull force), corner grip durometer testing (Shore 60A rubber), vent channel spacing for thermal clearance ≥2mm.
05
Testing, Validation & Quality Gates Drop test: 1.5m onto concrete (pass threshold: no functional damage after 10 drops). Magnet retention: ≥800g lateral force before detach. Grip adhesion: zero slip at 45° incline with 300g load. Dimensional tolerance: ±0.5mm. Thermal: surface temp ≤42°C at 1-hour MiFi operation in stand mode.
06
Market Launch & Commercialisation DTC (direct-to-consumer) online launch via e-commerce platform. Low-volume (≤50 units): handcast by artisan team, QC inspected individually. High-volume (500+ units): injected PU and moulded rubber via certified manufacturer, batch QC sampling per AQL 2.5 standard.

08 / 10 — 8M'S FRAMEWORK

8M's Analysis

Systematic root-cause and operational framework for LINKPOD™ production

The 8M's Framework (an extension of the Ishikawa cause-and-effect methodology) provides a structured lens across all operational dimensions — ensuring quality, consistency, and scalability whether producing 10 units by hand or 10,000 units at a manufacturing facility.

Manpower
(Man)
Low volume: Artisan cast team — 2–3 skilled craftspeople trained in PU leather wrap, silicone casting, and quality inspection. Each unit hand-finished, individually QC'd.
High volume: Factory operators per standard work instructions (SWI). Operator certification required for injection moulding and PU lamination. QA inspector ratio 1:50 units minimum.
Training: Onboarding via visual work instructions (VWI); competency assessed before production clearance.
Machine
(Equipment)
Low volume: Silicone casting molds (CNC-machined aluminium), heat press for PU leather bonding, ultrasonic welding pen for seam finishing.
High volume: Injection moulding press (clamping force 120T min), CNC router for mould tooling, automated PU lamination line.
Calibration: Press temperature calibrated ±2°C; mold wear inspected every 500 cycles. Magnet insertion jig ensures positional accuracy ±0.3mm.
Material
(Inputs)
PU Leather: 1.2mm PU-coated microfibre, REACH-compliant, UV-stable, minimum 50,000 Martindale rub cycles.
Rubber: Shore 60A vulcanised rubber, RoHS-compliant, minimum 200% elongation at break (ASTM D412).
Magnets: N45 neodymium, nickel-plated (corrosion-resistant), 1.2 kg rated pull force tested per ISO 3611.
Lining: 400gsm microfibre — scratch-protective, anti-static.
Adhesive (V1 kit): 3M VHB 5952 — 1,000 N/m² shear strength.
Method
(Process)
Low volume process: (1) Silicone core cast → (2) PU leather cut and heat-bonded → (3) Magnet/hook insert → (4) Lining adhesion → (5) Edge finish → (6) Individual QC inspection → (7) Pack and dispatch.
High volume process: Follows ISO 9001:2015 quality management system. Stage-gate inspection at: raw material receipt, post-moulding, post-assembly, final QC. AQL 2.5 sampling plan applied. Non-conforming units tagged and quarantined via NCR process.
Measurement
(Metrics)
Dimensional: Cavity tolerance ±0.5mm (caliper-verified). Wall thickness ≥2.5mm. Vent channel width 2.0mm ±0.2mm.
Mechanical: Magnet pull ≥1.2 kg (digital force gauge). Corner hook grip ≥300g lateral at 45°. Drop resistance: 10× from 1.5m (no functional failure).
Thermal: Surface ≤42°C after 60min MiFi operation (IR thermometer).
Durability: PU rub cycles ≥50,000 (Martindale). Rubber snap test ≥5,000 open/close cycles.
Weight: PU version ≤38g. Rubber version ≤42g.
Environment
(Mother Nature)
Materials: REACH-compliant PU and rubber. No phthalates, no heavy metals. Recyclable packaging (FSC-certified kraft box, soy-based inks).
Production: Low-volume casting produces near-zero waste (off-cuts reground). High-volume factory required to meet ISO 14001 environmental management standard.
Longevity: Product lifespan target ≥5 years — reducing accessory waste cycle. Replaceable lining insert extends product life further.
Shipping: Carbon-neutral last-mile delivery option offered at checkout.
Management
(Systems)
Production governance: Each batch tracked via batch code + production record. Digital QC log maintained per unit (low vol) or per batch (high vol).
Supplier management: Minimum two qualified suppliers per critical material (magnet, PU, rubber) to prevent single-source risk.
Version control: Design changes managed via ECN (Engineering Change Notice) — all modifications approved before production release.
KPIs monitored: First Pass Yield (FPY) target ≥97%, Customer Defect Rate (CDR) ≤0.5%, On-Time Delivery (OTD) ≥95%.
Maintenance
(Upkeep)
Moulds: Inspected every 500 cycles; cleaned after every run. Replaced at 10,000-cycle wear threshold.
Equipment: Preventive maintenance schedule (PM) — weekly cleaning, monthly calibration, annual third-party calibration certification.
Product care (customer): PU leather cleaned with damp microfibre. Rubber cleaned with mild soap. Magnets demagnetisation-checked annually if stored near strong fields.
Lining replacement: Microfibre lining available as a separate SKU — users can replace at 2–3 year mark without replacing the full unit.
Why the 8M's matter for LINKPOD™ The 8M's framework ensures quality is not dependent on any single variable — whether a unit is hand-cast by an artisan or produced at scale, every dimension of quality is accounted for, measured, and controlled. The same standard, at any volume.
