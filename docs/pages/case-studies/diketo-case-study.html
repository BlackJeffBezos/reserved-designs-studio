02 / 07 — THE CHALLENGE

The Challenge

Tradition fading in the age of screens

In many South African homes today, traditional indigenous games are slowly disappearing. Children are growing up surrounded by smartphones, tablets, and global entertainment, while the rich cultural games their parents and grandparents once played are fading into memory.

The core tension Parents want their children to stay connected to their African heritage — but they also want experiences that feel exciting, modern, and relevant in today's fast-moving world — not something that feels "old-school" or only for the village.

03 / 07 — THE SOLUTION

The Solution

Preserving the soul. Upgrading the form.

DIKETO was created with one clear purpose: to preserve African indigenous games and successfully pass them on to the next generation. By modernising the classic stone-tossing game without removing its soul, DIKETO brings new life to a beloved tradition.

The core gameplay remains unchanged Still built on hand-eye coordination, rhythm, and skill — the original magic is intact. What changed is the material, the glow, and the feel. Throw · Scoop · Catch · Repeat.
1
Throw Toss a glow stone into the air and scatter the remaining stones in the same motion. Hand-eye coordination begins immediately.
2
Scoop Before the thrown stone lands — scoop the target number of stones from the mat. Rhythm and precision are everything.
3
Catch Catch the falling stone in the same hand. Miss it, drop a stone, or move too slow — and your turn ends.
4
Repeat — and level up Levels increase the number of stones to scoop per throw. The player with the most stones when the round ends wins.

05 / 07 — EARLY RESULTS

Early Results

From hesitation to competition — every time

Across early use in multiple households and small group settings in Tsakane, Johannesburg, a clear pattern emerged. That transition — from hesitation to competition — became consistent.

First household test — Tsakane, Johannesburg The first round was chaotic — players overwhelmed by the new redefinition of the game, others who didn't even know it growing interest in the moment, rules being clarified in real time. Within minutes: focused, competing, correcting each other, and asking for "one more round."
